ABOUT CENTRAL ELECTRICITY BOARD

The Central Electricity Board (CEB) is a parastatal body and operating under the aegis of the Ministry of Energy and Public Utilities. Established in 1952 and empowered by the Central Electricity Board Act 1963, the CEB's business is to "prepare and carry out development schemes with the general object of promoting, coordinating and improving the generation, transmission, distribution and sale of electricity" .

FACILITIES

CEB (FACILITIES) Co. Ltd is a private company fully owned by the CEB. It is responsible for the operation of a Utility Call Centre which offers 24/7 call centre services to both CEB & CWA customers through their hotlines.


FIBERNET

CEB (FIBERNET) Co Ltd is a private company, fully owned by the CEB. It is licensed by the ICTA to provide Wholesale Data Services to licensed operators in Mauritius. It is leveraging on CEB’s extensive utility infrastructure (OPGW/ADSS) and METISS submarine international connectivity to encourage the deployment of world-class broadband infrastructure in the country.

CUSTOMER PORTAL

The Customer Service Portal of the CEB provides online customer services and facilities. Behind a secure online registration and login, customers are empowered to do online payment, calculate their bills, check their consumption or balance, and get their account statements. Register today and enjoy a new customer experience.


SUPPLIER PORTAL

The Supplier Portal facilitates procurement processes by connecting the CEB with suppliers in a secure, online environment. It provides a platform that allows suppliers to register online and download bidding documents.
